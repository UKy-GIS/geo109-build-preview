Good maps?

  • are persuasive.
  • They influence a reader's opinion.
  • Maps are arguments;
    • they make a statement
    • and provide data to support that statement.

Persuasive Cartography

  • Cartography: the art of map making, literally 'earth writing'
  • Propaganda vs. geographic information
  • Cornell online exhibition

Purposes of persuasion

  • Create and infuse new beliefs and ideas
    • A tourist map of downtown restaurants.

Purposes of persuasion

  • Reinforce current beliefs and ideas
    • A map of your property

Purposes of persuasion

  • Invalidate current beliefs and ideas
    • A map of COVID-19 rates

Purposes of persuasion

  • Instill doubt in current beliefs and ideas
    • A map of unrealized threats

Methods of persuasion

  • Appeal with data and rational arguments
  • Argue with 'facts'

    • How 'good' is your data?

    Map design

    • What projection and scale do you use?
    • What colors and fonts do you use?

    Narrative design

    • Are the map elements clear?
    • Do you compelling text and images in the layout?
    • Can the presentation be understood without your explanation?

    Map design

    • Color: create a visual hierarchy

      • Use convention when possible.
